Act

Find ways to get involved in your community.

Practical, nonpartisan ways to support civic engagement, help others access reliable information, and participate in local decisions.

Civic participation does not end with learning how government works.

The Act section is here to help you take the next step: getting involved in real, practical, nonpartisan ways.

Whether you are a student, educator, or community member, there are ways to support civic engagement, help others access reliable information, and participate in the decisions that affect your community.

Choose your path

For students For educators For community members

For students

Build civic engagement where you learn.

High school students

Get started at your school.

  • Start or join a civic engagement club
  • Help organize a nonpartisan voter registration drive
  • Share official voter registration and preregistration information
  • Invite local officials or election workers to speak at school
  • Encourage students to learn what offices and questions appear on local ballots
  • Join student council, youth advisory boards, or local youth commissions
  • Help classmates find official voting information without telling them how to vote

College students

Organize on campus.

  • Check registration rules and deadlines for your state
  • Learn whether you should register at home or at your campus address
  • Join or start a campus civic engagement group
  • Help run nonpartisan voter registration tables
  • Work with student government, faculty, or administrators to share voting information
  • Help students find polling places, mail ballot information, and deadlines
  • Encourage your campus to build long-term voter engagement plans

For educators

Help students practice civic life.

  • Teach students how local, state, and federal government connect
  • Explain what different offices actually do
  • Help students practice reading ballots and ballot questions
  • Share official voter registration and election information
  • Host nonpartisan classroom discussions about civic participation
  • Connect students with school board meetings, public hearings, or local government events
  • Support student-led voter registration or civic engagement projects
  • Use simulations, projects, and discussion-based learning to make civics feel practical

For community members

Participate beyond Election Day.

  • Check your voter registration status
  • Learn what offices and questions are on your ballot
  • Attend school board, city council, county, or public agency meetings
  • Read local meeting agendas before decisions are made
  • Contact elected officials about issues that matter to you
  • Volunteer with nonpartisan voter education or registration efforts
  • Help friends, family, or neighbors find official election information
  • Become a poll worker if eligible
  • Follow local government websites for public notices, meetings, and comment periods
