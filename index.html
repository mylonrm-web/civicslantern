About

A nonpartisan civic resource built to make participation easier.

Civics Lantern is a nonpartisan voter and civic education resource created by a high school student as an advocacy project for Glisten’s National Student Council.

The goal of Civics Lantern is simple: make civic information easier to find, understand, and use. Reliable voting and government information is often spread across many different websites, written in confusing language, or difficult to navigate for people who are just beginning to participate.

This project does not support or oppose any political party, candidate, campaign, or ballot position. Its purpose is to help people understand the systems around them, find official information, and participate in their communities in informed, practical ways.

Nonpartisan commitment

Civics Lantern is designed to be educational, not persuasive. The site may explain how elections work, where to find deadlines, or how to contact officials, but it does not tell users who to vote for or what position to take.

Accuracy and updates

Civic information can change. Election deadlines, officeholders, district maps, voting rules, and official links may be updated by states, counties, courts, legislatures, or election offices.

Disclaimer

Civics Lantern is an independent educational project. It is not an official government website and does not provide legal advice. Information on this site is intended for general civic education and voter resource purposes only. For the most current and legally binding information, users should check their official state election office, county election office, or other relevant government agency.
